About Me

I'm at Bath Uni for a PhD in Statistical Applied Mathematics, with a keen research interest in applied stochastic processes, particularly Lévy processes. Under the advice of prof. Andreas Kyprianou, I am currently exploring excursions from hyperplanes for stable processes.

My academic journey began with a Bachelor's degree in Actuarial Sciences from UNAM in Mexico, followed by a Master's in Probability and Statistics from the Center for Research in Mathematics (CIMAT) in Guanajuato, Mexico. During my undergraduate studies, I focused on variance estimation for complex survey sampling. At CIMAT, I delved into stochastic actuarial risk models and completed my thesis on the Gerber-Shiu function for a specific renewal risk model.

Professionally, I have gained valuable experience as a data scientist at The Nielsen Company. Additionally, I have worked as a consultant for organisations such as the Human Rights Commission of Mexico City and the Mexican Bureau of Culture. In these roles, I collaborated with interdisciplinary teams, including professionals from humanities and and social sciences, to provide comprehensive statistical analysis and insights. This diverse experience has equipped me with a well-rounded perspective and the ability to integrate quantitative methods with qualitative insights, making me a versatile and effective consultant.

I have extensive experience in tutoring and teaching, having served as a teaching assistant for probability and statistics courses at both undergraduate and postgraduate levels at the University of Bath, UNAM, and CIMAT. Additionally, I have been a lecturer of probability and risk theory at FES Acatlán. This background has honed my ability to convey complex mathematical concepts in a clear and engaging manner, and I am passionate about helping students develop a strong foundation in these subjects. In addition to my academic and professional interests, I play the flute and enjoy a good long run.

This page will primarily feature a portfolio of projects and entries to a blog. The last year of the PhD keeps me incredibly busy, so I rarely have time to post here. However, I will make an effort to share insights and interesting developments whenever I can. If you want a corporative version of this 'About Me' my CV in the link below.
